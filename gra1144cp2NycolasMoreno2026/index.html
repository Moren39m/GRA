Ajiaco Recipe

Colombian Ajiaco is one of the most traditional soups; its origins can be traced to pre-hispanic and colonial traditions. Ajiaco is a chicken soup full of rich flavors, where color, texture, and Creole ingredients add to its complexity as a traditional dish. There are different versions of Ajiaco, but in these variations, the soup always contains Chicken, three kinds of potatoes, corn, and a herb name guascas as its main pillars. This soup is most commonly prepared in the mountain regions of the country and served in a clay pot. This recipe will focus on the most popular version: the Bogotano Ajiaco or Santafereño.

Ingredients

“Some ingredients might be replaceable with similar variants, but others such as the guascas herb are indispensable and might only be found on Latin stores.”
  • 4-6 Cups of water (1000 - 1420 ml)
  • 1 ½ Chiken flavor bouillion (18 g)
  • 1 Chicken breast skinless with bone (400 g)
  • ¼ Pound of colombian yellow creole potatoes (or yellow potato as replacement) peal and cut into slices (125 g)
  • ¼ Pound of pastusa potatoes (or white potato as replacement) peal and cut into slices (125 g)
  • ¼ Pound of Andian potato (or red, white potato as relplacement) peal and cut into slices (125 g)
  • 2 Corns slice by the middle (380 g)
  • Bunch of guascas herbs
  • Bunch of scallions
  • Bunch of cappers
  • Garlic
  • Bunch of cilantro
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • 4 Spoons of heavy cream
  • 1 Medum size avocado cut in 4 portions

Steps

  1. In a large pot add chicken, slice corn, chicken bouillon, garlic, bunch scallions, bunch cilantro, then add water, bring to boil, then reduce to medium heat till chicken is tender and cooked between 20 to 30 minutes.

  2. Once chicken is cooked remove and set aside let to cool and then proceed to shred it. Remove the buch cilantro, and bunch of scallions. set aside and dice into pieces, then add them back into the pot.

  3. Add the sliced and peeled potatoes, this is what will thicken the broth as the yellow Creole potatoes will dissolve as they cook into the broth.

  4. Add guascas herbs whether freshs or dried, add them in two pieces cook till the broth thickens, and the potatoes are soft. this procees takes between 15 to 20 minutes.

  5. Serve the Ajiaco hot nect to sliced avocado, heavy cream, capers are serve as taste on the soup.
